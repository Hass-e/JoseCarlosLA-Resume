About me

I graduated as an Electronic Engineer from the Autonomous University of Baja California, and I'm currently working as a Manual QA Engineer. While I enjoy manual testing and the attention to detail it requires, I’ve found that what really excites me is automating processes — especially when it helps make things more efficient and reliable.

Since starting in the QA field, I’ve always wanted to go a step further and learn new skills. That curiosity led me to explore test automation, and now I spend much of my free time experimenting with it at home. I mainly use WebdriverIO or Cypress with JavaScript/TypeScript, and I run my tests through Jenkins to follow good CI/CD practices as closely as possible.

Beyond what I learned at university, I consider myself very much self-taught when it comes to programming/developing. I’ve picked up solid working knowledge of HTML and CSS, which helps me better understand how web pages behave — and how to automate them effectively.

I’ve been focusing heavily on JavaScript/TypeScript, not just because it’s a powerful tool, but also because there’s such a supportive developer community behind it. It makes learning more enjoyable and problem-solving a lot more accessible.

Lately, I’ve also started learning Python with Selenium, and even though I still consider myself a beginner in this area, I’ve been surprised by how quickly I’ve been able to make progress. The syntax feels intuitive, and I’m becoming more confident in writing scripts, understanding core concepts, and exploring its potential for automation and testing.

My interest in automation really started at a previous job, where I noticed the team lacked structure in their daily workflow. I saw an opportunity to build something useful, so I created a tool in Excel using VBA to help us keep track of tasks and improve how we worked together. That project was a turning point — it showed me how much I enjoy building solutions that make processes smoother and smarter.
That learning oporunity made me realize that I wanted to keep on doing this kind of work and now I find myself learning and digging more and more into this fields every day.

These days, I find myself more and more drawn to automation. It feels like the natural next step after manual testing, and I’m excited to keep growing in this area. I'm currently looking for an opportunity where I can continue learning, contribute with the skills I’ve built so far, and keep pushing myself forward as an Automation QA Engineer.
